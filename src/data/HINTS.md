# Exercise Hint Guidelines

## The Core Rule
**A hint must never contain the answer.**

If a kid reads the hint and can immediately fill in the blank or pick the correct option, the hint is too good.

---

## Hint Quality Levels

### ✅ Good hints
- Give a strategy or thinking process: *"Zähle in 3er-Schritten."*
- Point to a concept without naming the answer: *"Maskuline Nomen haben welchen Artikel?"*
- Ask a guiding question: *"Sprich das Wort laut. Welchen Laut hörst du zuerst?"*
- Reference related knowledge: *"Denk an: der Baum, der Apfel..."* (doesn't give the exact answer)

### ❌ Bad hints
- Arrow patterns: `"Hund ist maskulin → der"` → reveals the answer directly
- Full conjugation tables: `"ich habe, du hast, er hat"` → answer is "hat"
- Complete calculations: `"20-6=14"` → IS the answer
- Split words: `"Ap-fel – zwei Silben"` → makes the question trivial
- Sequence ending at answer: `"3, 6, 9, 12 → 4 mal"` → answer is 4

---

## Rules per Exercise Type

### Multiple Choice (Artikel)
❌ `"Hund ist maskulin → der"`
✅ `"Hund ist ein maskulines Nomen. Welcher Artikel gehört zu maskulinen Nomen?"`

### Fill-in-Blank (Verb conjugation)
❌ `"ich + essen → esse"`
✅ `"Bei ich endet das Verb meist auf -e. Probier es laut aus!"`

### Fill-in-Blank (Math)
❌ `"20-6=14"` or `"Addiere: 30+20=50, 4+5=9 → 59"`
✅ `"Rechne Zehner und Einer getrennt, dann addiere zusammen."`

### Silben (Syllable counting)
❌ `"Ap-fel – zwei Silben"` (shows the split = trivial to count)
✅ `"Sprich das Wort langsam und klapp bei jeder Silbe."`

### ABC / Alphabet
❌ `"A, B, C, D, E ..."` when answer is E
✅ `"Sing das Alphabet bis dahin. Was kommt nach D?"`

---

## General Guidelines
1. **Max 2 hints per exercise** — if you need 3, the question is too hard
2. **First hint = strategy**, second hint = slightly more specific (but never the answer)
3. **Never write the answer in the hint**, even partially
4. **For Grade 1**: hints can be more concrete (kids are 6-7), but still shouldn't give away the answer
5. **For Grade 2-3**: hints should teach the rule, not the specific answer
